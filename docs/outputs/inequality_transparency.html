Secrecy and Inequality Analysis

Investigating the relationship between transparency and cooperation in unequal settings

Project Overview

This analysis explores the relationship between financial secrecy and inequality, examining how transparency and punishment mechanisms affect cooperation in public good contributions. The study provides insights into how institutional structures can promote fairness and social welfare in unequal settings.

Key Findings

  • Transparency paired with peer punishment significantly increases cooperation
  • Advantaged participants show higher willingness to pay for secrecy
  • Punishment effectiveness depends on transparency of endowments
  • Income inequality persists and can increase without proper transparency mechanisms

Methodology

The analysis employed experimental economics methods, using a public goods game with varying levels of endowment transparency and punishment mechanisms. Statistical analysis includes confidence interval estimation and hypothesis testing to ensure robust findings.
