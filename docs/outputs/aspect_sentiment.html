Enhancing Taxpayer Experience: Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis of IRS Chatbot Interactions

Project Overview

This comprehensive analysis focused on the IRS's high-volume public chatbot, processing millions of taxpayer interactions annually. Using advanced Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA), we systematically identified specific drivers of taxpayer satisfaction and frustration, delivering actionable insights for measurable improvements in chatbot effectiveness and user experience.

Methodology Highlights

  • Data Foundation: 100,000 anonymized chatbot transcripts (Jan-May 2024)
  • Advanced NLP: Hybrid approach combining domain expertise with Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)
  • Sentiment Analysis: NLTK's VADER for nuanced sentiment scoring per interaction aspect
  • Cross-functional Collaboration: IRS Account Management, IT, Privacy, Legal, and UX teams

Key Findings

  • Critical UX Issue: "Ease of Use" showed 88.8% negative sentiment despite high task completion rates
  • Clarity Challenges: "Clarity of Instruction" carried 28.6% negative sentiment
  • Task-Specific Pain Points: "Understanding Tax Forms" (20.6% negative) and "Making Tax Payments" (19.3% negative)
  • Validation Success: Strong correlation between automated sentiment analysis and self-reported satisfaction
Overall Sentiment Distribution
Initial analysis revealed that while majority interactions are positive, nearly 1 in 4 are neutral or negative, highlighting significant improvement opportunities.
Continuous Sentiment Analysis
Detailed sentiment distribution reveals distinct clusters around neutral and negative experiences, reinforcing the need for granular analysis.
Sentiment Validation
Strong correlation between automated transcript sentiment and self-reported satisfaction validates our ABSA methodology as an accurate UX measurement tool.
Top Taxpayer Tasks
"General Inquiry" dominates at 41.2%, highlighting critical need for effective intent recognition and triage capabilities.
Sentiment by Task Type
Task-specific analysis reveals "Understanding Tax Forms" and "Making Tax Payments" as high-friction areas requiring immediate attention.
Conversation Aspects Analysis
Critical diagnostic insights: "Ease of Use" shows 88.8% negative sentiment even when "Resolution Success" is 99.3% positive, revealing hidden UX problems.
Voice of the Taxpayer - Positive Themes
Positive feedback emphasizes efficiency ("quick," "efficient"), successful resolution ("solved," "worked"), and ease of use ("easy").
Voice of the Taxpayer - Negative Themes
Negative feedback highlights unresolved issues ("didn't work," "failed"), accuracy concerns ("provided wrong"), and trust erosion.

Strategic Impact & Recommendations

Immediate Actions

  • Ease of Use Overhaul (High Priority): Investigate and redesign problematic flows due to extreme negative sentiment
  • Clarity Enhancement: Refine responses for "Clarity of Instruction" and "Form Guidance" with simpler language
  • Task-Specific Improvements: Focus UX efforts on "Understanding Tax Forms" and "Making Tax Payments"
  • Feedback Loop Implementation: Address negative feedback themes like broken links and accuracy issues

Business Value

This analysis provided a framework for systematic UX improvement that moves beyond overall satisfaction scores to identify specific, actionable intervention points. The methodology demonstrated measurable ROI through targeted improvements in user experience while maintaining high task completion rates.

Methodology & Technical Approach

Our rigorous approach centered on Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA), allowing us to dissect conversations into meaningful components and assign sentiment to specific aspects rather than overall interactions. This precision enabled targeted improvements with measurable impact on customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Success required unified execution across IRS Account Management, IT, Privacy, Legal, UX teams, and Database Analysts. This collaborative approach ensured research directly served operational priorities through regular stakeholder reviews and clear success metrics.
