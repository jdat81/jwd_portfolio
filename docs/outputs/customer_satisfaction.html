Customer Satisfaction Analysis

Project Overview

This analysis delves into customer satisfaction metrics across different service touchpoints, identifying key drivers of satisfaction and areas for improvement. The study combines quantitative CSAT scores with qualitative feedback to provide actionable insights for service enhancement.

Key Findings

  • Overall CSAT score shows positive trend with 85% satisfaction rate
  • Response time emerged as the primary driver of customer satisfaction
  • Digital service channels outperform traditional channels by 15 percentage points
  • Customer feedback analysis revealed opportunities in process simplification

Methodology

The analysis employed a mixed-methods approach, combining statistical analysis of CSAT scores with text analytics of customer feedback. Visualization created using Plotly emphasizes temporal trends and comparative performance across service channels. Statistical significance tested at 95% confidence level for all key findings.
