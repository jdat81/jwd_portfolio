Willing to Pay: A Reasonable Choice Approach to Tax Compliance

Project Overview

This comprehensive experimental study examines the psychological and economic factors driving tax compliance decisions across diverse populations and institutional contexts. Using controlled laboratory experiments and field interventions, we investigate how individual motivations, social norms, and institutional design influence citizens' willingness to contribute to public goods through taxation.

Research Design Highlights

  • Cross-National Study: Multi-country experimental design comparing compliance behavior across different tax systems
  • Treatment Variations: Systematic manipulation of enforcement mechanisms, social information, and incentive structures
  • Behavioral Measures: Real monetary decisions combined with attitudinal surveys and demographic analysis
  • Sample Size: Over 1,200 participants across multiple experimental sessions and countries
  • Mixed Methods: Quantitative experimental data integrated with qualitative interview insights

Key Research Findings

  • Treatment Effects: Significant variations in compliance rates across different experimental conditions and intervention types
  • Cross-National Patterns: Notable differences in baseline compliance behavior between countries, reflecting institutional and cultural factors
  • Demographic Variations: Systematic gender and age-based differences in tax compliance motivations and behavior
  • Social Norms Impact: Strong influence of perceived social norms and peer behavior on individual compliance decisions
  • Intrinsic Motivation: Evidence for non-monetary drivers of tax compliance beyond deterrence effects

Evidence-Based Tax Policy

  • Behavioral Interventions: Social norm messaging and peer comparison effects show promise for improving voluntary compliance
  • Institutional Design: Findings inform optimal audit strategies and penalty structures for different populations
  • Cultural Adaptation: Cross-national variations suggest need for culturally-adapted compliance strategies
  • Gender-Responsive Policy: Systematic gender differences indicate potential for targeted intervention approaches

Practical Applications

This research provides tax authorities with evidence-based insights for designing more effective compliance programs. The findings demonstrate how behavioral science principles can be systematically applied to improve tax collection while maintaining taxpayer trust and voluntary cooperation.

Rigorous Experimental Design

Our approach employed carefully controlled laboratory experiments with real monetary incentives, ensuring authentic decision-making while maintaining strict scientific controls. The experimental design allows for causal inference about the effectiveness of different policy interventions.

Technologies & Statistical Methods

  • Data Analysis: Python (Pandas, NumPy) for data processing and statistical computing
  • Statistical Testing: SciPy for hypothesis testing and confidence interval estimation
  • Visualization: Seaborn and Matplotlib for publication-quality graphics and data presentation
  • Experimental Software: z-Tree for controlled laboratory implementation
  • Statistical Models: Mixed-effects regression accounting for clustering and repeated measures

Cross-Disciplinary Approach

The study integrates insights from behavioral economics, public finance, and social psychology to provide a comprehensive understanding of tax compliance behavior. This interdisciplinary approach ensures both theoretical rigor and practical relevance for policy makers.
